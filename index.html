THE PROJECT
Our project will provide the Birmingham Civil Rights Institute a web-based interactive map to demonstrate how movement played a part in activism through oral histories, photographs, and mapping of routes used in protests.

Project Proposal

The Birmingham Civil Rights Institute (BCRI) supplies two separate traveling exhibits: “The Freedom Rides Photographs” and “Selma-to-Montgomery: A March for the Right to Vote.” These two exhibitions highlight the ways in which movement -- both walking and riding -- contributed to the power and influence of African American civil rights activism of the 1960s throughout the South. We propose the design of an interactive map showing the routes of both freedom rides and significant civil rights marches, like the Selma to Montgomery march, while highlighting specific moments of the journey along the way using location pins, photos, videos, and oral histories from the BCRI Oral History Project collection.

As they exist currently, these exhibits -- focused on traveling and traversing as a key component to activism -- are designed to travel among organizations. Thus, the creation of an interactive map, highlighting the routes of these marches and rides, further acknowledges the significance of moving within civil rights movements. Listening to the oral histories immerses the user in the moment of time the movement is taking place in a way traditional pictures alone cannot. We want people to get a sense of being able to ‘walk in their footsteps’. Additionally, this map can be used by those interacting with the Civil Rights Institute website, and also has the potential to be implemented within the museum itself on touchscreen kiosks, to make these significant journeys in history more accessible to users both within and outside the museum.

About


The Team

Amish Vampires in Space is a diverse group of women combinbing their efforts to bring the messages of our original civil rights activists to today's diverse population. We are all doctoral students at Michigan State University working through the CHI-Matrix fellowship. Elise is in the Writing, Rhetoric, and American Cultures program and focuses on queer and feminist rhetorics. Julia is in the Information & Media Studies and focuses on online activism. Nicole is in the Anthropology department and focuses on the use of cultural heritage management within indigenous populations.


Our Collaborators

Duke University Libraries are partners in this project, allowing the use of their oral history archives, "Behind the Veil". The Southern Poverty Law Center and the NAACP also partnered with us to ensure both that the appropriate histories are exhibited on our map and that the content contained is not only sensitive to racial and political issues, but is accurate and encompassing of the civil rights movement as a whole. The Birmingham Civil Rights Institute is our primary partner through the integratation of two of their existing exhibits into a unique format.
